How to Submit a Shareholder Proposal

Submitting a shareholder proposal involves several key steps. Below are basic instructions to help you navigate the process and find the right contacts within the company.

  1. Review the Company's Guidelines: Visit the company's investor relations website or refer to their latest proxy statement to find specific guidelines and deadlines for submitting shareholder proposals.
  2. Understand Regulatory Requirements: Familiarize yourself with rules like SEC Rule 14a-8, which outlines eligibility criteria for proposals.
  3. Determine Eligibility: Ensure you meet ownership requirements, such as holding a minimum number of shares for at least one year.
  4. Identify the Correct Contact: Find the company's Corporate Secretary or Investor Relations department. This information is typically available in the company's proxy statement or on their investor relations website. Searching "Corporate Secretary [Company Name]" can help.
  5. Prepare Your Proposal: Customize the provided template to align with the company's guidelines, including word limits and formatting rules.
  6. Include Proof of Ownership: Attach a letter from your broker or bank verifying your ownership of the company's stock.
  7. Submit the Proposal: Send your proposal and proof of ownership to the Corporate Secretary via certified mail or a trackable delivery method before the deadline.
  8. Follow Up: Request confirmation of receipt and address any feedback promptly.

Shareholder Proposal: Assessment of Bitcoin Adoption

Proposal Summary

We request that the Board of Directors commission a report, to be made available to shareholders by a reasonable date, assessing the potential benefits and risks of adopting Bitcoin as a part of the company's financial strategy, including but not limited to holding Bitcoin on the corporate balance sheet and accepting Bitcoin for goods and services.

Supporting Statement Introduction:

In light of the evolving global financial landscape and the emergence of digital assets, it is imperative for [Company Name] to evaluate innovative financial strategies that could enhance shareholder value.

1. Comparative Corporate Performance:
  • MicroStrategy (MSTR): As of November 2024, MicroStrategy holds approximately 331,200 bitcoins, valued at over $30 billion. The company's stock has appreciated nearly 500% in 2024, showcasing Bitcoin's significant impact on shareholder value.
  • Tesla, Inc. (TSLA): Tesla's investment of $1.5 billion in Bitcoin and acceptance of Bitcoin as payment for its products have highlighted the mainstream corporate adoption of digital assets.
  • Block, Inc. (formerly Square): Block integrates Bitcoin into its financial services, including its popular Cash App. This strategic move has positioned the company as a leader in fintech innovation.
  • Marathon Digital Holdings: As a top Bitcoin mining company, Marathon has expanded its operations and holdings, contributing to a positive impact on its stock performance and market positioning.
2. Inflation Hedge and Store of Value:
  • Economic Context: Rising inflation rates have eroded the purchasing power of traditional currencies, emphasizing the need for assets like Bitcoin.
  • Bitcoin's Role: With a capped supply of 21 million coins, Bitcoin serves as a deflationary asset, providing a hedge against inflation.
  • Historical Performance: Over the past decade, Bitcoin has consistently outperformed traditional inflation hedges like gold.
3. Institutional Adoption and Market Maturation:
  • Regulatory Developments: Approval of Bitcoin Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) and clearer regulatory frameworks have legitimized Bitcoin in institutional finance.
  • Corporate Integration: Major corporations and financial institutions are integrating Bitcoin into their operations, indicating a significant shift in market acceptance.
  • Risk Mitigation: Enhanced liquidity and market infrastructure have reduced operational risks associated with Bitcoin transactions.
4. Fiduciary Responsibility:
  • Maximizing Shareholder Value: As fiduciaries, the Board and management have a duty to consider strategies that enhance shareholder returns.
  • Diversification Benefits: Bitcoin's low correlation with traditional assets can improve the risk-adjusted returns of the company's investment portfolio.
  • Innovation Leadership: Proactively exploring Bitcoin adoption positions [Company Name] as an industry innovator.
Conclusion:

By conducting a thorough assessment of Bitcoin's potential benefits and risks, [Company Name] can make informed decisions that align with its strategic objectives and fiduciary duties. We urge the Board to consider this proposal seriously and provide shareholders with their findings.
