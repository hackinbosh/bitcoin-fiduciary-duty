Shareholder Proposal: Assessment of Bitcoin Adoption

Proposal Summary

We request that the Board of Directors commission a report, to be made available to shareholders by a reasonable date, assessing the potential benefits and risks of adopting Bitcoin as a part of the company's financial strategy, including but not limited to holding Bitcoin on the corporate balance sheet and accepting Bitcoin for goods and services.

Supporting Statement

Introduction

In light of evolving global financial landscapes and the emergence of digital assets, it is imperative for [Company Name] to evaluate innovative financial strategies that could enhance shareholder value.

Justifications for Bitcoin Adoption

1. Inflation Hedge and Store of Value
  • Economic Context: With unprecedented monetary expansion and rising inflation rates, traditional currencies are losing purchasing power.
  • Bitcoin's Role: Bitcoin has a capped supply of 21 million coins, making it a deflationary asset by design.
  • Evidence: Historical data shows Bitcoin's appreciation over the past decade, outperforming traditional inflation hedges like gold.
2. Comparative Corporate Performance
  • Peer Benchmarking: Companies like MicroStrategy and Tesla have allocated portions of their treasury into Bitcoin.
  • Stock Performance: MicroStrategy's stock price has significantly appreciated since its Bitcoin acquisition strategy, potentially outpacing [Company Name]'s stock performance.
  • Shareholder Value: Bitcoin holdings have contributed to increased shareholder wealth in these companies.
3. Institutional Adoption and Market Maturation
  • Regulatory Developments: Approval of Bitcoin Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) and clearer regulatory frameworks are legitimizing Bitcoin in institutional finance.
  • Corporate Acceptance: Major corporations and financial institutions are integrating Bitcoin into their operations and service offerings.
  • Risk Mitigation: Increased liquidity and market infrastructure reduce the operational risks associated with Bitcoin transactions.
4. Customer and Market Demand
  • Demographic Trends: Growing consumer interest in digital currencies, especially among younger demographics.
  • Competitive Advantage: Accepting Bitcoin for goods and services can attract new customers and open up additional revenue streams.
  • Global Reach: Bitcoin facilitates cross-border transactions with lower fees and faster settlement times compared to traditional banking systems.
5. Fiduciary Responsibility
  • Maximizing Shareholder Value: As fiduciaries, the Board and management have a duty to consider all viable strategies to enhance shareholder returns.
  • Diversification Benefits: Bitcoin's low correlation with traditional assets can improve the risk-adjusted returns of the company's investment portfolio.
  • Innovation Leadership: Proactively exploring Bitcoin adoption positions [Company Name] as an innovator and leader in its industry.
Options for Consideration

Treasury Allocation:
  • Option A: Allocate a small percentage (e.g., 1-5%) of corporate treasury reserves to Bitcoin.
  • Option B: Use excess cash flows to gradually build a Bitcoin position over time.
Payment Acceptance:
  • Option A: Begin accepting Bitcoin as payment for products and services.
  • Option B: Partner with payment processors to convert Bitcoin payments instantly to fiat currency, mitigating volatility risk.
Strategic Partnerships:
  • Option A: Collaborate with fintech companies specializing in blockchain and cryptocurrency solutions.
  • Option B: Invest in Bitcoin-related startups or funds to gain indirect exposure.
Risk Management

  • Volatility Mitigation: Utilize hedging strategies and dollar-cost averaging to reduce the impact of price fluctuations.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Implement robust compliance frameworks to adhere to all applicable laws and regulations.
  • Security Measures: Adopt industry best practices for digital asset custody and cybersecurity.
Conclusion

By conducting a thorough assessment of Bitcoin's potential benefits and risks, [Company Name] can make an informed decision that aligns with its strategic objectives and fiduciary duties. We urge the Board to consider this proposal seriously and provide shareholders with their findings.

Optional Additions

1. Case Studies
  • MicroStrategy: Invested over $4 billion in Bitcoin since August 2020, leading to substantial appreciation in shareholder value.
  • Tesla: Purchased $1.5 billion worth of Bitcoin and began accepting it for vehicle purchases (temporarily suspended due to environmental concerns but open to resuming).
2. Environmental Considerations
  • Sustainable Mining Practices: Bitcoin mining is increasingly utilizing renewable energy sources.
  • Carbon Offset Strategies: Companies can offset their Bitcoin-related carbon footprint through investments in green initiatives.
3. Employee Incentives
  • Bitcoin Compensation: Offer employees the option to receive a portion of their salary or bonuses in Bitcoin.
  • Education Programs: Provide resources to educate employees about digital assets and blockchain technology.

Guidance Notes

  • Proposal Length: Keep the main proposal within the word limit specified by regulatory bodies (e.g., 500 words as per SEC Rule 14a-8 in the United States).
  • Deadline Compliance: Submit the proposal before the company's deadline for shareholder proposals, typically outlined in the previous year's proxy statement.
  • Regulatory References: Cite relevant laws and regulations that support the legitimacy of your proposal.
  • Professional Tone: Maintain a respectful and formal tone throughout the proposal.
